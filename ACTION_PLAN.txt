MAVEN FUZZY FACTORY — SPRINT 1 CONCLUSIONS
================================================================================

QUESTION: Who is coming to this site and what are they doing?

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CONCLUSION 1 — Repeat users are worth more, but only if they're on desktop
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Repeat desktop users convert at 10–11% vs 8–9% for new desktop users.
Revenue per session is $6.20–6.64 for repeat desktop vs $4.53–5.69 for new.
On mobile this advantage almost disappears — repeat mobile CVR is 3.42% vs
3.15% new. Loyalty is not overcoming the mobile experience problem.

→ Retention efforts should target desktop users. Retargeting repeat mobile
  users will underperform until mobile is fixed.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CONCLUSION 2 — Mobile is losing the business ~$105k in recoverable revenue
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Desktop CVR is 8–11%. Mobile CVR is 3% across every channel and user type.
gsearch mobile alone has 92,472 sessions. Closing half the gap to desktop
(3.15% → 5%) = ~1,750 extra orders × ~$60 AOV = ~$105,000 from one fix.
This is not a traffic quality problem — the same channels perform 3x worse
on mobile. It is a UX/product problem.

→ Mobile experience is the single highest ROI fix available.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CONCLUSION 3 — Socialbook has zero retention value and should be cut
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Socialbook CVR: 4.99% desktop, 0.83% mobile. Revenue/session: $3.22 desktop,
$0.57 mobile. repeat_pct = 0.0% across every funnel stage for socialbook.
Not a single socialbook user comes back for a second session.
Compare to gsearch desktop: 8.27% CVR, $4.93 revenue/session.

→ Cut or heavily reduce socialbook. Reallocate to gsearch desktop.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CONCLUSION 4 — The product page is where the most money is being lost
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
57,750 desktop gsearch sessions abandoned at the product page — the largest
drop-off point in the entire funnel. Users are interested enough to click
a product but not convinced enough to add to cart. This is a copy, imagery,
or trust signal problem, not a traffic problem.

→ Priority 1 fix. A/B test copy, images, social proof on product pages.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CONCLUSION 5 — Billing page is losing loyal users at the last step
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
10,514 gsearch desktop sessions abandoned at billing. Of direct traffic
billing abandoners, 65.94% are repeat users. These are people who know
the brand, came back, went through the entire funnel — and still didn't
complete. This is friction or payment UX failure, not a trust issue.

→ Audit payment options, form UX, error states. An abandoned billing
  email to repeat users would have high recovery rate.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CONCLUSION 6 — Mobile users are bouncing before seeing any product
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
51,565 mobile gsearch sessions bounced at stage 1 — the single largest
segment in the entire dataset. Mobile has two separate problems:
(1) users bounce immediately off the landing page
(2) users who do progress convert at 3x lower rate than desktop
These need two separate fixes.

→ Mobile workstream must address landing page AND checkout separately.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SPRINT 1 PRIORITY LIST
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Fix product page         — highest volume drop, all channels affected
2. Fix mobile experience    — 51k bounces + 3x CVR gap = two problems
3. Fix billing page         — losing loyal repeat users at the final step
4. Cut socialbook           — zero repeat users, worst CVR, worst revenue/session

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SPRINT 2 TO INVESTIGATE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- A/B test results via utm_content (landing page variants already in the data)
- Cohort retention curve (do users actually come back month over month?)
- Product-level analysis (which product has highest refund rate / cross-sell?)
- Revenue per session trend over time (is the business improving or plateauing?)
